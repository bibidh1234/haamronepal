Welcome to Patan

Patan

Patan, also known as Lalitpur, is one of the three ancient royal cities in the Kathmandu Valley. Renowned for its rich artistic heritage, Patan boasts a wealth of temples, palaces, and courtyards that reflect the grandeur of the Malla kings. Patan Durbar Square, with its intricately carved temples and traditional architecture, offers a fascinating glimpse into Nepal’s artistic brilliance.

This historic city is not only famous for its architectural splendor but also for its vibrant local culture and craftsmanship. With its beautiful courtyards and traditional artisans, Patan stands as a living museum of Nepal’s artistic and spiritual traditions.

Top Things to Do in Patan

  • Explore Patan Durbar Square: Discover ancient temples, palaces, and courtyards in this UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • Visit the Hiranya Varna Mahavihar (Golden Temple): A stunning Buddhist monastery known for its golden facade and intricate details.
  • Admire the Mahabouddha Temple: Famous for its intricate brickwork, this temple is known as the "Temple of a Thousand Buddhas."
  • Patan Museum: Dive into the city's artistic history with a visit to the museum, located in the old royal palace.
  • Shop at Local Craft Stores: Pick up traditional handicrafts and art pieces from Patan’s famous local artisans.
  • Explore the Kumbheshwar Temple: One of the oldest temples in Patan, dedicated to Lord Shiva, with a unique five-tiered pagoda structure.

Local Cuisine

Patan offers a rich culinary experience with local Newari dishes and traditional Nepali food. Be sure to try:

  • Newari Khaja: A traditional Newari platter featuring beaten rice, grilled meat, and a variety of side dishes.
  • Yomari: A sweet dumpling made with rice flour, filled with molasses and sesame seeds.
  • Choila: A spicy grilled meat dish, typically served with beaten rice and local pickles.
  • Samay Baji: A traditional Newari ceremonial dish with a combination of rice, beans, meat, and pickles.
  • Local Tea and Coffee: Enjoy Nepali tea and coffee in cozy cafes around Patan.
