Welcome to Mustang

Mustang

Mustang, often referred to as the "Last Forbidden Kingdom," is a remote region in Nepal that offers an otherworldly blend of stunning natural beauty and ancient Tibetan culture. Tucked in the northern part of the country, Mustang is known for its vast, arid landscapes, breathtaking Himalayan views, and centuries-old monasteries. This isolated region, once a part of the Tibetan kingdom, provides an opportunity to experience preserved Tibetan traditions.

Upper Mustang is the highlight of the region, featuring the legendary walled city of Lo Manthang. This ancient city, with its royal palace, monasteries, and hidden caves, offers a glimpse into a world untouched by time. Visitors need a special permit to explore this restricted area, adding to its mystique.

Mustang is also popular for trekking, offering a unique landscape of desert-like terrain, deep gorges, and towering cliffs, contrasting with the snow-capped peaks of the Himalayas in the background. The region’s remoteness and spiritual significance make it a top destination for adventurers and culture enthusiasts alike.

Top Things to Do in Mustang

  • Explore the walled city of Lo Manthang and its royal palace
  • Visit the centuries-old monasteries, including Tsarang and Jampa Lhakhang
  • Trek through Mustang’s unique desert landscapes
  • Discover ancient cave dwellings that date back thousands of years
  • Experience traditional Tibetan culture and festivals
  • Admire the dramatic landscapes of the Kali Gandaki Gorge

Where to Stay

Mustang offers a range of accommodations, from guesthouses in villages to boutique hotels in Lo Manthang. Here are some popular options:

  • Hotel Lo Manthang
  • Mustang Eco Resort
  • Red House Lodge
  • Karma Guest House
  • Tea houses along the trekking routes

Local Cuisine

While in Mustang, don’t miss the opportunity to try some of the local dishes, many of which are influenced by Tibetan culture:

  • Thenthuk: A Tibetan-style noodle soup with vegetables and meat
  • Tsampa: A roasted barley flour dish often mixed with butter tea
  • Momos: Dumplings filled with meat or vegetables
  • Yak cheese and butter tea
  • Traditional Nepali Dal Bhat (lentil soup with rice and vegetables)
