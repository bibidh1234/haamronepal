Welcome to Chitwan

Chitwan

Nestled in the southern Terai plains, Chitwan is a haven of biodiversity and natural beauty. Chitwan National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is renowned for its dense jungles, sprawling grasslands, and rich wildlife. It offers a unique opportunity to experience Nepal’s wild side and immerse yourself in nature.

Established in 1973, Chitwan National Park is home to a variety of wildlife including the elusive Bengal tiger, the one-horned rhinoceros, and a diverse range of bird species. The park’s diverse ecosystems, from dense forests to open grasslands, provide critical habitat for its wildlife.

One of the main attractions in Chitwan is the thrilling jungle safari. Whether you choose to explore the park on a jeep safari, an elephant ride, or on foot with a naturalist guide, you’re bound to encounter fascinating wildlife and breathtaking landscapes. The park also offers opportunities for canoeing on the Rapti River, where you might spot crocodiles and enjoy the tranquil surroundings.

In addition to its wildlife, Chitwan is also known for its Tharu culture. The Tharu people, indigenous to the Terai region, offer a glimpse into their traditional lifestyle through cultural performances and village tours. Their unique customs and vibrant festivals add an extra layer of cultural richness to your visit.

Top Things to Do in Chitwan

  • Embark on a jungle safari in Chitwan National Park
  • Enjoy a canoe ride on the Rapti River
  • Experience traditional Tharu dance and cultural performances
  • Visit the Elephant Breeding Centre
  • Explore the park’s diverse flora and fauna on a nature walk
  • Discover the rich birdlife with a guided bird-watching tour
  • Relax in the serene environment of a riverside resort

Local Cuisine

Chitwan’s cuisine reflects the rich flavors of the Terai region. Don’t miss out on these local dishes:

  • Tharu Cuisine: Traditional dishes of the Tharu people, including fish curry and various vegetable preparations.
  • Dal Bhat: A staple Nepali meal consisting of rice, lentil soup, and vegetables.
  • Newari Snacks: Spicy and tangy snacks popular in the region.
  • Local Drinks: Refreshing beverages like fresh fruit juices and traditional Nepali tea.
