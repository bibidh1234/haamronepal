Welcome to Bhaktapur

Bhaktapur

Bhaktapur, an ancient city located in the Kathmandu Valley, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site renowned for its well-preserved medieval architecture and vibrant cultural traditions. Often referred to as the “City of Devotees,” Bhaktapur offers visitors a unique glimpse into Nepal’s rich history and cultural heritage.

The city is known for its stunning temples, palaces, and courtyards that date back to the 12th century. The Bhaktapur Durbar Square, with its ornate pagoda-style temples and intricately carved woodwork, is a highlight of the city and a testament to the architectural brilliance of the Malla kings who ruled over Bhaktapur.

Strolling through Bhaktapur’s narrow streets, you’ll encounter traditional Newari houses, bustling marketplaces, and local artisans crafting pottery and woodwork. The city’s rich cultural festivals, such as Bisket Jatra and Indra Jatra, provide an opportunity to witness vibrant processions, traditional dances, and local ceremonies.

Bhaktapur is also famous for its local delicacies, including the delicious "king curd" (juju dhau), a must-try treat for visitors. The city’s traditional architecture, lively festivals, and welcoming atmosphere make it a captivating destination for anyone interested in exploring Nepal’s heritage.

Top Things to Do in Bhaktapur

  • Explore the Bhaktapur Durbar Square and admire its historic temples and palaces.
  • Visit the 55-Window Palace and the Vatsala Temple.
  • Wander through the ancient streets and observe traditional Newari architecture.
  • Experience the lively atmosphere of local festivals and cultural events.
  • Try the famous juju dhau (king curd) and other local delicacies.
  • Discover the artisans’ workshops and witness traditional crafts being made.
  • Relax at the serene surroundings of the Taumadhi Square.

Local Cuisine

Bhaktapur’s culinary scene is rich and varied, reflecting the city’s cultural heritage. Don’t miss these local specialties:

  • Juju Dhau: A rich and creamy traditional curd, known as the “king curd.”
  • Bhaktapur’s Local Newari Cuisine: Includes dishes like momo (dumplings) and chatamari (rice crepe).
  • Dal Bhat: A traditional Nepali meal consisting of lentil soup, rice, and vegetables.
  • Local Snacks: Such as kachori (deep-fried pastries) and bara (spiced lentil patties).
  • Traditional Teas: Refreshing herbal and spiced teas available in local cafes.
